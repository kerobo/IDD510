Esopus Creek Conservancy

The Esopus Creek Conservancy (ECC) is located in Saugerties, New York, a Hudson Valley town bordering the Hudson River and the Catskill Mountains, one hundred miles north of New York City. While Saugerties is their home base, their purpose extends beyond Saugerties' boundaries and involves protecting the Esopus Creek watershed.

Established in July 2003 as a nonprofit corporation, ECC is among the newest land trusts in the State of New York. Working with the Catskill Center for Conservation and Development and enjoying remarkable private and public support, they have acquired a 156-acre natural environment. The property shares a mile of shoreline with the upper Esopus Creek in Saugerties. Its name - Esopus Bend Nature Preserve.

That's just the beginning. Going forward, the Conservancy will provide public access to the preserve, create hiking trails and offer educational programs. Working with landowners and other environmental groups, they support the protection of additional environmentally sensitive properties.

The Esopus Creek Conservancy welcomes, indeed encourages, your involvement and support.

Kiwanis Club of Saugerties

"KIWANIS" is a thriving body of dynamic, service-minded individuals of all ages. In all, more than 600,000 members in more than 75 countries are "Serving the Children of the World" through their involvement in the Kiwanis family.

The name was coined from an expression of the American Indians in what is now the Detroit area. In the Otchipew language, the expression "Nunc Kee-wanis" meant broadly: "we trade" or "we share our talents".

Since 1921, the Kiwanis motto has been "We Build". This motto captures the spirit in which Kiwanis Clubs contribute significantly to their communities.

Kiwanis is one of the most successful and active clubs in Saugerties. In addition to the Hudson Valley Garlic Festival™, the Kiwanis Club of Saugerties sponsors, plans and puts on a Mothers Day Rose Sale and the July 4th celebration (parade and fireworks) that Saugerties has become renowned for.

Thanks to funds from these events, worthwhile events can be funded and contributions can be made to deserving groups and efforts such as the Kiwanis Club Ice Arena, the Girl Scouts, the Boy Scouts, the Boy's and Girl's Clubs, Saugerties Area Council of Churches, Saugerties High School student scholarships, the Saugerties Mum Festival, the Key Club, the Builders Club, the American Cancer Society, the American Legion, the Blue Kats, Children's Annex, Diaz Ambulance, the Woodstock School of Art, the Saugerties Christmas tree lighting, and the "Coats for Kids" program.

For more info on the Kiwanis Club, visit saugertieskiwanisclub.com

Saugerties Fish and Game Club

Established in 1884, the Saugerties Fish and Game Club is one of the oldest sportsmen's clubs in New York State. Located on 100 acres of land off Fish Creek road in the town of Saugerties, the club is open year-round, with a large parking area and large heated clubhouse with a fully-equipped kitchen.

Facilities include:

  • 100-yard rifle range with sheltered shooting stations and several bench rests.

  • 25-yard pistol range with 12 sheltered shooting stations, heated in winter. Separate area for shooting falling plates.

  • 2 ATA-certified trap ranges, each with 5 stations.

  • 3-D walk-through archery range, and bale range.

  • Woods-walk black powder course.

Saugerties Lions Club

Lions Club International began as the dream of Chicago insurance man Melvin Jones. He believed that local business clubs should expand their horizons from purely professional concerns to the betterment of their communities and the world at large.

Jones organized a meeting of business groups from around the country and on June 7, 1917, the group met and declared themselves the “Association of Lions Clubs”. The group has since developed into an international organization with nearly 1.4 million members in 189 countries worldwide.

The Lions club motto is “We Serve” and this motto was challenged in 1925 by Helen Keller who asked the Lions to become “knights of the blind in the crusade against darkness”. Since that time sight preservation has been the priority of the Lions Club. The Lions, however, support their communities in any way they can.

The Saugerties Lions club is the oldest service organization in Saugerties. The club was chartered in 1932 and has been serving Saugerties ever since through fund raising activities such as annual Auctions, Golf Tournaments, Casino Bowling, Baskets of Cheer and an occasional Las Vegas Night.

The proceeds from these events are used to support and upkeep the Frank Greco Lions Playground and wading pool on Washington Avenue, provide $4000 annually in college scholarships, fund vision and hearing services for those otherwise unable to afford them, hold an annual Easter Egg hunt for local children, donate to many local children’s sports teams, contribute to The Saugerties Council of Churches Christmas Toy Store and numerous emergency situations.

The Lions are always looking for likeminded individuals to help with community service. If you are interested in joining, call one of the numbers above.

Saugerties Unit of Kingston Boys and Girls Club

The mission of the Boys & Girls Clubs of Kingston, Saugerties Unit is to work towards helping youth of all backgrounds with special concern for those from disadvantaged circumstances, develop the qualities needed to become responsible citizens and leaders.

Our philosophy is that there should be a positive safe place for youth in the community where they can develop ongoing relationships with caring adults and other youth.

Programs will be offered in the following core program areas: Character and Leadership Development, Education and Career Development, Health and Life Skills, the Arts, and Sports, Fitness and Recreation. Active participation should lead to youth developing a sense of competence, usefulness, belonging, power and influence.

In its short history, The Boys & Girls Club has gone from an idea of a youth center to the reality of finally getting a “home of our own.” We will offer three different programs throughout the year; after school, “The Club”, and summer.

Programs:

Main Club

A dedicated drop-in youth facility that services ages 7 to 18 years old. This year round clubhouse offers age appropriate programs that range from Home Work Help, Art Education and Creation, Nutritional Cooking Classes, Health and Life Skills, Leadership Groups, Computer Lab Courses, and Sports and Recreation opportunities.

After School Extension Programs

Licensed New York State childcare programs offered at the Grant D Morse and Riccardi Elementary Schools. Children go directly to the program following school and get picked up at 6PM. Snacks are provided daily. Programs include Power Hour, Triple Play and Smart Moves.

Ulster Free and Accepted Mason (F&AM) Lodge

Located in the scenic Hudson Valley, Ulster Lodge #193 is located in the Village of Saugerties.

Ulster Lodge #193 is a Regular and Recognized Masonic lodge located in Saugerties, NY chartered in 1848, having a charter through the Grand Lodge of NY, Free and Accepted Masons (F&AM).

Free and Accepted Masonry is one of the oldest catalogued fraternities. Founded on the principles of morality, charity and brotherly love, Free Masonry has endured the test of time. Though it is not a religion, nor is it affiliated with any one particular religion, it does however encourage spiritual growth on an individual basis.

Freemasons give on average $2 million dollars per day to different charities across the world. Some of the local charities that Ulster Lodge #193 contributes to are: 1-support the food pantries. 2- To provide Thanksgiving and Holiday/Christmas dinners for the poor and needy. 3- To provide support for other non-profits organizations, shut-ins, the elderly and children in need. 4- to help support children’s sports and activities in the area.
