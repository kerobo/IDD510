15 Minutes of Frame

What do Brooklyn, Woodstock and Saugerties have in common? Well, for one thing, the "Fifteen Minutes of Frame" artwork of Ze'ev Willy Neumann.

Visit the Village Beach, where you will have the opportunity to see, touch, sit on, or otherwise experience the latest frame to be installed by Mr. Neumann, in places where one can enjoy beautiful surroundings framed by his large art pieces.

In Mr. Neumann's own words: "An artist's role is to break preconceived notions built up in our lifetime, and to give us a fresh look at things we have grown accustomed to. A frame hearkens back to an old understanding we have of art, static paintings hanging on a wall. However, in our modern lives, through television, computers, even books and magazines, we use the frame to help us concentrate on our immediate purpose and focus. The intent of my sculpture is to bring attention to the forms, contrasts, rhythms, and colors within the frame, and around it. The viewer's ability to participate in the work, by posing with it in various ways, reminds us that what is within the frame is only a small part of the bigger picture.

Between the Tides Festival

Celebrate the historic Saugerties Lighthouse, our beloved local landmark of the Hudson River Valley! Every summer, the not-for-profit Saugerties Lighthouse Conservancy hosts a music festival fundraiser to support the continued preservation of this vital beacon.

The festival is scheduled “between the tides” so the half-mile trail to the Lighthouse is passable throughout the event. It's a family-friendly, all-ages event including a full line-up of live music and a rich buffet of local food and drinks. The Lighthouse museum and tower are also open to visitors as part of festival admission.

No pets allowed during the festival, please.

Fees:

Advanced tickets & seniors: $25/person. Day of event: $30. Children under 12 are free.

Tickets go on sale starting July 1. For additional details, please call the Lighthouse at 845-247-0656, 9am to 5pm.

Directions:

For complete directions, please visit Saugerties Lighthouse website

Decks and Docks on the Esopus

Decks and Docks events have become a fun part of the Saugerties busy event schedule. Participants come by kayak, rowboat, auto, bike, and on foot to explore the Esopus Creek and Esopus Bend Nature Preserve.

Visitors are welcomed to share their decks, docks, lawns, and patios with enthusiasm and generous offerings of food and drink. One participant, looking forward to future events, summed it up: ". . . a splendid introduction to the unexpected beauties of some watery corners of Saugerties, and the opportunity to make some fascinating new friends."

Come on out, in June (see the Calendar for specifics/rain date), to show your support for the Esopus Creek Conservancy (ECC). Come by boat, come by car, ride your bike, walk or hike . . . come delight in the creek and the flora, fauna, and neighbors who call it home.

By popular demand, last year's concert creek-side at Esopus Bend Nature Preserve will be repeated for the added enjoyment of "Decks and Docks" visitors.

Esopus Creek Puppet Suite

The Arm-of-the-Sea Theater's annual mask and puppet theater adventure on the Esopus Creek shores, in Saugerties.

Each year, contemporary themes are explored through the ancient traditions of mask and puppet theater. Puppet figures, mask characters and performing objects of all sizes and description will take center stage when Arm-of-the-Sea Theater and friends present their annual Esopus Creek Festival Suite.

The festival is a celebration of puppetry's ancient roots and its emergence as a contemporary performance art form. It features original shows by Arm-of-the-Sea Theater and other theater artists of the region. Arm-of-the-Sea Theater is a not-for-profit organization based in Saugerties, NY, in the beautiful Hudson River Valley.

For over twenty years Arm-of-the-Sea has been developing a style of magical realism that marries the mythic and the everyday, humanity and the earth, visual arts and natural science, poetry and politics.

Their large-scale productions incorporate ancient theatrical devices for shared enchantment and visual storytelling. With music, gesture and stunning visuals, they illuminate the links between human communities and the life-support processes of this living planet.

HITS on the Hudson

With the Catskill Mountains as its backdrop, HITS-ON-THE-HUDSON is a beautiful place to spend the day. The world-class facility is owned and managed by HITS, Inc., the largest producer of Hunter/Jumper horse shows in the country, and holds seven weeks of competitions between May and September each year. Host to some of the world's most prestigious equestrian events, the facility has quickly become an important venue in the equestrian world. On average, the weekly attendance at the 200+ acre show grounds is 2,000 horses and 5,000 horsemen.

Equestrians, as well as spectators with little or no knowledge of the sport, come to enjoy the beautiful surroundings, and a day of watching impressive athletes compete in thrilling events. With numerous classes competing in ten rings simultaneously, spectators always have several exciting events to choose from. All the rings have grass and bleacher seating, and the facility is handicapped accessible. Merchants at a diverse array of indoor and outdoor shops and booths offer unique jewelry, gifts, clothing, accessories, and souvenirs that will appeal to horsemen and non-equestrians alike. Diners can enjoy an international menu at HITS Bar & Grill, either in the casual dining room, or on a shaded patio that offers an unobstructed view of the warm-up rings.

Activities:

For a schedule of events, visit the HITS Saugerties Web Site or see the Saugerties Village Web Calendar on this site.

Fees:

Generally, the events are free during weekdays and occasionally there is a nominal charge of $5 for weekend attendance/parking. (The money for weekend attendance is donated to Family of Woodstock.)

Hudson Valley Garlic Festival

The Hudson Valley Garlic Festival™ is in its 25th year, and is a fun-filled celebration of the garlic harvest, presented by the Kiwanis Club of Saugerties. All proceeds go to worthwhile community causes.

The festival is usually held the last full weekend of September.

The location is Cantine Field in Saugerties (Mile Marker 101, NY State Thruway).

Activities:

Your day will include:

A visit to the Garlic Marketplace, with tons of gourmet garlic.

The Food Alley, featuring garlic-enhanced foods. There’s sausage, pulled pork, garlic soup, garlic green beans, and even garlic ice cream! Something for everyone, from vegetarian to serious carnivores.

Constant entertainment, varying each year between bluegrass, Cajun, ragtime, Irish music, and other music . . .

Cooking demonstrations by renowned chefs of the Hudson Valley.

Instructive garlic growing and braiding talks, including members of the Garlic Seed Foundation.

An Arts and Crafts area featuring over 80 vendors.

A Children’s Tent with activities for the younger kids and a climbing wall for older children.

A poster contest. You get to vote, to help choose next year’s winning poster design.

Fees:

Tickets are $10 at the gate for a single-day pass. Children under 12 are admitted free, when accompanied by an adult.

Advance tickets are available for $7, at select locations, and for $5 for seniors 65 years of age and older. See hvgf.org for details.

Mum Festival

The 2016 Mum Festival will be held on Sunday, October 9th. (Rain date: Sunday, October 16th.) You can take a look at the beauty and fun to be enjoyed at the mum festival by perusing the photos below. For a larger image of any of the picture, click on the thumbnail images.

Calling All Volunteers

The Mum Festival Committee is looking for new members. If you are interested, please contact Jeannine Mayer at jmmayer@hvc.rr.com.

The committee meets monthly from June until the festival date.

Activities:

The Mum Festival weekend actually begins with the preceding Mum Bowl, the Saugerties High School football game, where the coronation of the Mum Queen and her Court takes place.

The following day, Sunday, the festival begins at Noon and runs to 5:00 PM. It typically includes:

  • The Mum Queen and her court presenting flowers courtesy of the Saugerties Society of Little Gardens

  • Live music (band and chorus)

  • An art show

  • Children's activities

  • Free horse rides

  • Local vendors

  • Refreshments

So come wander Seamon Park's paths, take in its views, and visit the bandstands & fountains. You'll perhaps never see as many "Kodak moments" as you will during your Mum Festival visit and any other visit to the park during the year.

And of course, the thousands of Mums that decorate Seamon Park for the Mum Festival will be available for viewing for the remainder of the fall.

This year will be the 51st annual Mum Festival, and the 107th anniversary of Seamon Park. A brief history - John Seamon generously donated the beautiful park to the Village of Saugerties in 1809. The Mum Festival committee has decided to honor the park's special anniversary at this year's Mum Festival and will be celebrating with old time music, Campfire Girl exhibits, Wildlife shows, and other activities that highlight the park's 107-year history.

Saugerties Artists Studio Tour

Each year, participating Saugerties artists and artisans open their studios to the public for a weekend. Hundreds of visitors become privy to the process of art creation in dozens of unique studios situated in village and country locations. Painters, sculptors, printmakers, potters, illustrators and more invite people into their studios where they may see work in progress, completed works, and even have a hands-on experience!

Most artists offer work for sale, often at very affordable prices. For detailed descriptions and samples of artists work, visit their Web site: saugertiesarttour.com.

Saugerties Farmers Market

The Saugerties Farmers Market was established in 2002 to help stem the loss of farmland by addressing the economic needs of farmers and to support the historic village business center of Saugerties.

The summer season (May - October) of the market is held on Saturdays, from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m., 2 blocks from the Main/Partition intersection . . . in the Cahill School Parking Lot, next to the historic Kiersted House (115 Main Street).

The market has a wide variety of produce including organic vegetables; luscious orchard fruits and berries; artisan bread; fresh laid eggs; delicious honey, jams & sauces; pasture raised poultry & meat; mushrooms; wine; fresh herbs; field-grown flowers; and tasty local cheeses; ice cream; breads and other baked goods. Products change seasonally. Take home freshly harvested food grown, picked, and brought to market by local farmers.

Where do the Winter Holiday Markets take place?

Indoors at the Senior Citizens Center (207 Market Street)

“What will you have to sell in the winter?”

Plenty, including beef, lamb, pork, smoked fish, turkey, chicken, eggs, baked goods (pie, cake, cookies, sweet breads), bread, sprouts, mesclun, garlic, herbs, root vegetables (potatoes, carrots, onions), winter squash, frozen peak-of-season vegetables, apples, pears, dried fruits, dairy (cheeses, milk, yogurt), mushrooms, wine, cider, granola, maple syrup products, honey, jarred goods (jam, sauces, chutneys, pickles), pesto, pot pies, chili, casseroles, soups, stock, prepared foods, snacks, and more.

Dates for the Winter holiday markets are November through April. See the Farmers Market Web Page for date/time specifics.

Activities:

In addition to a wide variety of market goods, you'll also experience chef demonstrations, free tastings and recipes.

"Arts in the Marketplace" includes musicians, artists, and puppeteers to entertain and delight.

Many weeks, there will be entertainment from musicians who used to play in the John Street Jam, as well as Cornell Cooperative farming experts on hand to offer growing advice.

Kids can participate in planned events. Check the Saugerties Farmers Market calendar for Special events throughout the market season.
